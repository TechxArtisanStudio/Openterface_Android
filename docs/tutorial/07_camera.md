# Tutorial 7 — Camera and Video Controls

**Level:** Beginner · **Prerequisites:** Tutorial 3 · **Estimated time:** 15 minutes

This tutorial covers everything related to the video feed: format, image adjustments, orientation, screenshots, and recording.

---

## 7.1 Video Format

Open the settings panel (Menu button) and tap **Video Format** to choose:

- **Resolution** — higher resolutions show more detail but use more bandwidth. Common options: 1920×1080, 1280×720, 640×480
- **Frame rate** — higher FPS gives smoother video. Common options: 30fps, 60fps

**Tip:** If the video is choppy or freezes, try a lower resolution or frame rate. The available options depend on what your Openterface device supports.

---

## 7.2 Image Adjustments

In the settings panel, tap **Controls** to adjust the camera's image parameters:

| Slider | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Brightness** | Make the image lighter or darker |
| **Contrast** | Increase the difference between light and dark areas |
| **Hue** | Adjust the color balance |

These adjustments happen in real-time. Move a slider and watch the video preview respond immediately.

---

## 7.3 Rotating the Video

If your target computer's display is oriented differently from your phone:

| Button | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Rotate 90 CW** | Rotate the video 90° clockwise |
| **Rotate 90 CCW** | Rotate the video 90° counter-clockwise |
| **Flip Horizontally** | Mirror the video left-to-right |
| **Flip Vertically** | Mirror the video top-to-bottom |

These are useful when the Openterface device is mounted upside-down or sideways.

---

## 7.4 Screenshots

Tap **Screen Capture** in the settings panel to save a snapshot of the current video frame.

The screenshot is saved to your device's default media folder. Make sure the app has **Storage permission** for this to work.

---

## 7.5 Video Recording

Tap **Record Video** in the settings panel to start recording the video feed:

- A **red recording indicator** with a timer appears at the top of the screen
- The button color changes to show that recording is active
- Tap the button again to **stop** recording
- The video is saved to your device's default media folder

**Use cases:**
- Record the target machine's boot process
- Capture error messages or troubleshooting sessions
- Document configuration steps for reference later

---

## 7.6 Picture-in-Picture (PiP)

The app supports a secondary camera view that appears as a small thumbnail:

- The thumbnail shows in the top-left area of the screen
- Use the **drag handle** to reposition it
- This is useful if you have a second camera connected
