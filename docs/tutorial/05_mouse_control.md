# Tutorial 5 — Controlling the Target Mouse

**Level:** Beginner · **Prerequisites:** Tutorial 4 · **Estimated time:** 10 minutes

Learn how to use your phone screen to control the mouse on the target computer.

---

## 5.1 Absolute Mode (Default)

This is the simplest way to control the mouse:

- **Tap** the screen → the cursor jumps to that exact position on the target and **left-clicks**
- The position maps proportionally: if you tap the center of your phone, the cursor moves to the center of the target's display

**Example:** If the target is running at 1920×1080 and you tap the center of your 1080×2400 phone screen, the cursor moves to position (960, 540) on the target.

---

## 5.2 Relative Mode

Works like a laptop trackpad:

- **Drag** your finger → the cursor moves by the same relative distance
- **Lift** your finger → the cursor stays where it is
- Drag again from anywhere to continue moving

This mode is useful when you need to move the cursor a small distance without tapping the exact spot on your screen.

---

## 5.3 Absolute Drag Mode

Combines absolute positioning with click-and-drag:

- **Tap and hold** → the cursor jumps to that position and clicks
- **Keep your finger down and drag** → the cursor follows your finger
- A **"Drag"** label appears on screen to remind you that you're in drag mode
- **Lift** your finger → the cursor releases

Use this for:
- Dragging files between folders on the target
- Selecting blocks of text
- Drawing or resizing windows

---

## 5.4 Mouse Buttons

| Action | Result |
|---|---|
| **Single tap** | Left-click |
| **Long press** | Right-click (may depend on settings) |
| **Double tap** | Double-click |

---

## 5.5 Tips for Accurate Mouse Control

- **Start with Absolute mode** — it's the fastest for most tasks
- **Use Relative mode** for fine adjustments — moving the cursor a few pixels at a time
- **Switch to Drag mode** when you need to hold the mouse button while moving
- **Edge tapping** — the app maps screen edges to screen edges. Tap the very corner to reach the very corner of the target display
- **Zoom in** with a two-finger pinch to make small targets easier to tap precisely

---

- [ ] You can reach any position on the target screen
