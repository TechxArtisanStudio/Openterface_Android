# Tutorial 4 — Understanding the Main Screen

**Level:** Beginner · **Prerequisites:** Tutorial 3 · **Estimated time:** 10 minutes

You're connected and the app is running. Let's walk through everything on the screen so you know what each part does.

---

## 4.1 Layout Overview

```
+---------------------------------------------------+
|                                                   |
|                                                   |
|                                                   |
|      [ Live video from the target computer ]      |
|                                                   |
|      (tap anywhere = click at that spot)          |
|                                                   |
|                                                   |
|                                        [Keyboard]  |
|                                        [  Menu  ]  |
+---------------------------------------------------+
```

| Area | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Video preview** | Shows the target's screen. This is also your touch surface — tap to click, drag to move. |
| **Keyboard button** | Opens and closes the on-screen keyboard. |
| **Menu button** | Opens and closes the settings panel. |

---

## 4.2 The Video Preview Is Your Window

Everything you see in the video preview area is a live feed of the target computer's display. Whatever the target is showing — desktop, BIOS, terminal, browser — appears here in real-time.

The preview area is also your **control surface**:

- **Tap** = left-click at that position
- **Tap and drag** = move the mouse (in drag mode)
- **Two-finger pinch** = zoom in/out on the preview

---

## 4.3 Floating Action Buttons (FABs)

Two buttons float at the bottom-right corner of the screen:

### Keyboard Button (⌨)

Tap to toggle the on-screen keyboard. When open, the keyboard appears above the buttons with a full QWERTY layout, modifier keys, function keys, and shortcuts.

Tap again (or the **X** on the keyboard) to close it.

### Menu Button (☰)

Tap to open the side settings panel. The panel slides in from the right side with options for device management, camera controls, mouse mode, and more.

Tap the empty area outside the panel (or the menu button again) to close it.

---

## 4.4 Mouse Control Modes

The app offers three ways to control the mouse. You can switch between them in the settings panel.

| Mode | How It Works | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Absolute (Default)** | Tap anywhere and the cursor jumps there and clicks. Fast and direct. | Most tasks |
| **Relative** | Drag your finger and the cursor moves relative to your drag, like a laptop trackpad. Your finger can lift and the cursor stays put. | Fine cursor positioning |
| **Absolute Drag** | Tap and hold, then drag. The cursor follows your finger while held. A "Drag" label appears. | Dragging files, selecting text |

**Tip:** Start with **Absolute** mode. It's the most intuitive — what you tap is where the cursor goes.
